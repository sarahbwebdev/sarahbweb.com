WHO HAD IT FIRST?

In 2019, there were 63 names common for both boys and girls. Based on the years they first ranked in the top 1000 for each gender, 53 were names for boys later given to girls, and only 10 were popular for girls before being adopted for boys. Most often, unisex names are established when a girl is given a boy's name and other parents follow suit. So if you're expecting a girl, you could also take a look at the names for boys.

LATE 20TH CENTURY

The baby boom kicked off in 1946 following World War II, and it was in full force in the 1950s, so the boomers own the names of the 50s, and half the names in the 60s as well. The culture that evolved in the 1960s plus an increasingly diverse society had an effect on names in the decades that followed. Names created by African-Americans began appearing on the top lists.
